Erecura is the Queen of Dis, the second layer of Hell, and the wife of its ruler, the archdevil Dispater. She is a demigoddess whose origins lie in mortality — she was condemned to Hell for stealing the secret of eternal life from Pharasma, the goddess of death and fate. Despite being an authority figure within Hell, her outlook and motivations are far removed from those of typical devils, and remain unclear even to her own husband.

As part of her nuptial agreements with Dispater, Erecura is allowed to keep a palace of her own liking — the lush, green-filled Eleusys — and to exercise power as she sees fit within Dis. Dispater sincerely loves her, often calling upon her to arbitrate disputes and delegating responsibilities of rule to her even when she does not ask. Asmodeus himself plans to rely on Erecura's savvy should Hell enter conflict with forces beyond its ordinary ken.

Her divine portfolio encompasses deduction, mind reading, subtlety, thriving in hostile environments, and the art of manipulating dangerous beings to one's advantage. Her worship is virtually unknown on Golarion and is strongly tied to the veneration of Dispater. Those few who do follow her tend to be soothsayers and non-evil rangers who admire her ability to maintain integrity and power within an environment of overwhelming evil.

Erecura prefers green and tan robes within her own palace of Eleusys, and dons black in her public role as Queen of Dis. She is attended by three norns — Felixia, Rowane, and Selieste — who serve as her advisors and handmaidens.
