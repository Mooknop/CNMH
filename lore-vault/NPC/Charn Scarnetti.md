Charn has spent seventeen years watching the inheritance he considers his by right erode: the mill murder, the manor's ruin in the giant attack, the collusion scandal with the Bunyip Club. He blames his father's mismanagement and, above all, the meddling of adventurers. His campaign of fear against Abstalar's plan to call Nualia's spirit is equal parts insecurity and ambition — he means to build a name as a 'protector of the people,' and once adventurers start delving the Pit, his rumor-mongering finds a new target.

What Charn does not know is that the clockwork songbird's head he carries is a tether for the ghost of Jervis Stoot. Most days Stoot is only a voice at the back of his mind. But there are nights Charn cannot account for — nights he wakes exhausted, in clothes that are not the ones he went out in.
